about

Peter Forberg and Nick Earley are two Chicago friends traveling to Dakar, the capital city of Senegal, for the winter. While there, they’ll be taking courses on African Civilization and Wolof, the most widely spoken indigenous language. This is their blog.

When Peter Forberg walked into Northwestern Medicine’s Travel Clinic, the nurse described him as “a little guy” that Senegal is “going to eat right up.” Weighing in at 137 pounds, Peter is 70 inches of repressed masculinity and ALDI-brand thin mints. Friends describe him as trustworthy, aloof, determined, and inattentive. In his free time, he makes things like jam, horchata, earrings, poetry, video games, synth music, and embroidered button-up shirts with bears on them. He hopes to improve his foreign language skills, learn some new recipes, and fabricate clothing from scratch. If you’re in Chicago, check out his film series running at Doc Films on Thursday evenings. He doesn’t do college improv, and a 28-year-old German man is currently sleeping in his bed.

Nick Earley can write his own goddamn bio. Peter would do it as a joke, but he’s tired.
