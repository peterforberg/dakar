about

Peter Forberg and Nick Earley are two Chicago friends traveling to Dakar, the capital city of Senegal, for the winter. While there, they’ll be taking courses on African Civilization and Wolof, the most widely spoken indigenous language. This is their blog.

Peter Forberg is a third year sociology major. Friends describe him as trustworthy, aloof, determined, weird, and likely a closeted homosexual. In his free time, he makes things like jam, horchata, earrings, poetry, video games, synth music, and embroidered button-up shirts with bears on them. Armed with a laughable grasp on the French language and an impending nihilism wrought by his ex-girlfriend’s decision to cheat on him for 3 months, Peter is ready to take on a new country where he can escape the anxieties of American culture. He hopes to improve his foreign language skills, learn some new recipes, and fabricate clothing from scratch. If you’re in Chicago, check out his film series running at Doc Films on Thursday evenings. He doesn’t do college improv, and a 28-year-old German man is currently sleeping in his bed.

Nick Earley can write his own goddamn bio. Peter would do it as a joke, but he’s tired.
